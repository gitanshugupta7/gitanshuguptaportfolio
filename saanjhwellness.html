01

The Brief

A site whose first job is how it makes you feel.

The name does a lot of work before the design even starts. Saanjh means dusk, the part of the day when things slow down and soften, and that sets the whole tone. This is a wellness brand, so the site is not really there to list features or close a sale. It is there to make a person feel calm, looked after, and safe enough to take a first step.

That step is the whole point, and it is harder than it sounds. Someone arriving at a wellness site is often a little unsure, sometimes a little vulnerable. The brief, underneath everything else, was to lower the temperature: to make reaching out feel like an easy, low-pressure thing rather than a leap.

02

Approach

Designing for tone, not for features.

↳ THI · Gestaltung 1

Most of the thinking here went into a single idea: in wellness, people read tone before they read words. Before anyone parses what you offer, they have already decided how the page makes them feel, and that feeling is what they trust or distrust. So the design problem is emotional first and informational second.

The wellness sites that get this right share a quiet family of choices: soft, warm palettes, real and human imagery rather than stock perfection, generous space, unhurried motion, and writing that sounds like a person who cares. They avoid three things just as carefully, the clinical and cold, the loud and salesy, and the cluttered and overwhelming. The brief was to land squarely in that calm middle.

The real metric was not time on page. It was whether someone who felt hesitant left feeling a little more at ease.
03

Audience

Who arrives, and how they feel when they do.

This is the brand's likely audience rather than interviewed people, and worth confirming once Saanjh's exact focus is settled.

Someone looking for a gentler way forward

Curious, hopeful, and a little hesitant.

They have been thinking about this for a while before arriving. They are weighing whether this feels right and whether they will be judged. They respond to warmth and honesty, and they retreat from pressure. The site's job is to meet that hesitation kindly and make the next step small.

04

Information Architecture

Few choices, one gentle path.

↳ THI · Webdesign & Webusability

For a hesitant visitor, too many options is its own kind of stress. So the structure stays deliberately small and leads, without pushing, toward one calm action: understand what Saanjh is, feel that it is trustworthy, and then reach out when ready. Every page points softly at that same next step instead of scattering attention across a dozen calls to action.

05

Design

Lowering the heart rate, on purpose.

↳ THI · Gestaltung 1 · Design von MMI

Everything in the visual language points at calm. A warm, dusky palette drawn from the brand's own name, soft type with plenty of air around it, and imagery that feels human rather than staged. Motion stays slow and quiet, present only to make the experience feel gentle, never to grab.

The hardest discipline was knowing when to stop adding. A wellness site is one of the few places where doing less is unmistakably better, where empty space reads as care rather than as something missing. The aim was a page that feels like a held breath let out slowly.

06

Testing & QA

Calm has to hold everywhere, for everyone.

The quality work was the usual responsive and performance checks, with one thing pushed higher than normal: accessibility. If the people you are designing for may be tired, anxious, or simply not at their best, then legible type, calm contrast, and an interface that never fights back are not nice-to-haves, they are the point. A wellness site that is hard to use quietly contradicts itself.

07

Technical Implementation

Quiet on the surface, solid underneath.

↳ THI · Webtechnologien

The site was built with Django, Tailwind, HTML, and CSS, the same foundation as the rest of my work, with content kept editable so the brand can update its words and images without a developer in the loop. Nothing flashy under the hood, by design. The work that mattered here was on the surface, where the calm lives.

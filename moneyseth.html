Overview

A finance site that teaches the boring half of investing

MoneySeth is run by a group of finance professionals who want to help ordinary Indians invest better. I designed and built the platform end to end. Most retail finance sites chase the excitement of stocks, so MoneySeth took the opposite, more useful bet: explain bonds, debentures, and steady income clearly, and pair that with practical tools and plain-language guidance. The design job was to make a subject people find dry and intimidating feel approachable and trustworthy.

The problem

Retail India chases stocks and overlooks the bigger market

↳ THI · Studiendesign & UX-Tests

The debt market is actually larger than the equity market, yet almost all retail attention, and almost all retail content, goes to stocks. Bonds and NCDs, the instruments that give steady, lower-risk income, are barely explained anywhere a beginner would look. When they are, it is in jargon: coupon, tenure, SLR, NCD versus bond, with no one translating it.

So a new investor who wants something safer than stocks lands in a gap: the safe option exists, but the information is scarce, scattered, and intimidating. MoneySeth set out to be the place that closes that gap and earns trust by being clear.

Research

What a beginner actually needs to trust a finance site

↳ THI · Gestaltung 1 · Studiendesign & UX-Tests

This was category and content research rather than formal interviews, so I am describing what shaped the design, not invented findings. Two things stood out.

Clarity is the product. For a nervous first-time investor, plain language and worked examples matter more than data density. A site that defines its terms, answers obvious questions, and shows rather than tells will be trusted over one that assumes knowledge.

The bond gap is a content opportunity. Because stock content is everywhere and bond content is rare, owning the bond explanation is both a service to readers and a way to stand out and rank. That insight shaped the whole positioning.

Audience

Three readers, three reasons to come back

MoneySeth serves a spread of readers, and the structure has to hold all three. These are grounded in the site's evident audience, not interviewed personas.

The curious beginner

New to investing. Wants the basics in plain words: what a bond is, how it differs from a fixed deposit, where to start.

The active retail investor

Already investing. Wants current bond and NCD lists, IPO and index data, and calculators to plan returns.

The community follower

Follows the brand on YouTube and Telegram. Comes to the site for the deeper reference behind the videos.

Information architecture

Three jobs: learn, calculate, track

↳ THI · Webdesign & Webusability

The site is organised around the three things a reader comes to do. Learn, through the blogs and explainers. Calculate, through the calculator suite. Track, through the live bond, IPO, and index information. Accounts sit on top for people who want to save and return.

Grouping the navigation this way means each reader finds their path quickly: the beginner heads to the blogs, the planner to the calculators, the active investor to the data. It keeps a content-heavy site from feeling like a pile of links.

The defining decision

Specialising where the giants do not

The biggest decision was what to be about. The easy path was to be one more general stock-market site, competing with thousands of louder, better-funded ones. The better path was to own a corner no one served well: bonds, NCDs, and steady income.

Centering the site on bonds did three things at once. It gave readers something genuinely missing, it set MoneySeth apart instead of drowning it in a crowded category, and it built trust through depth, a site that explains the hard, unglamorous instrument well reads as more credible than one chasing hype. The bond focus is not a section of the site, it is the reason the site exists.

The tools

Calculators that turn readers into users

↳ THI · Design von MMI · Webtechnologien

Reading teaches, but tools get used and shared. MoneySeth offers a suite of calculators, EMI, SIP, compound interest, and recurring deposit, that let a visitor put their own numbers in and see a real answer. They turn a passive reader into an active user, give people a reason to return, and bring in steady search traffic from anyone looking to run the numbers on a decision.

Build & reach

A content platform, not a brochure

↳ THI · Webtechnologien

MoneySeth is a working platform, so it was built on Django with a Postgres database, and Tailwind, HTML, and CSS on the front end. The blogs, bond and IPO data, calculators, and user accounts are all dynamic, which lets the team keep the reference current without a developer. Beyond the site, the brand reaches readers through YouTube, Telegram, Instagram, and Twitter, and the site is the deeper home that content points back to.
